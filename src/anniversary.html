Care's Package Anniversary

70 years of Lasting Change

CARE was founded in 1945, when 22 American organizations came together to rush lifesaving CARE Packages to survivors of World War II. Thousands of Americans, including President Harry S. Truman, contributed to the effort.

The power of a box

In 1946, Regine Binet in Bayeux, France, opened a box that may have saved her life. It contained canned meat, dried milk and other rations. The CARE Package provided desperately needed food and supplies to starving families after World War II. It also carried hope. Regine’s was just one of 100 million CARE Packages delivered to the hands and homes of people across the globe — revealing the power of a box to change the world.

Retooling Surplus Rations to Feed a Starving Post-war Europe

By the end of WWII, Europe and Asia lay in ruins. And millions of U.S. Army ration packs lay unused in a warehouse in the Philippines. CARE purchased 2.8 million of them in early 1946. They were called “10-in-ones,” as each contained enough food to feed 10 men for a day or one man for 10 days — canned meat, raisins, chocolate and other items. Over time, customized CARE Packages better met the dietary preferences and requirements of diverse people.

CARE’s Evolution: Striking Poverty at its Roots

The CARE Package has evolved dramatically in 70 years and today delivers lasting change through tools that strike at poverty’s roots – and that can’t be contained in a box. We help villages start savings groups and families access health care that keeps them well. We help girls stay in school — and out of forced marriage. With CARE’s help, communities tap into clean water and proper sanitation, women start and grow businesses, farm their land, plan their families, feed their children, overcome disaster and weather a changing climate — revealing the power of today’s CARE Package to change the world.

Focusing on Women and Girls as a Catalyst for Change

Our programs empower communities tomorrow, while meeting the needs of people in crisis today. Through it all, we focus on women and girls, because they bear the brunt of poverty — and hold the key to defeating it. Empowered women create ripples of positive change that lift up everyone around them, including other women, girls, boys, men — entire communities.
